Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS)

The Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) is designed to develop a new generation of medical professionals with 21st-century healthcare skills. It combines theoretical learning in a revolutionary digital environment with hands-on practical rotations.

From the start of the first year, learners will spend a minimum of 3 hours per week of clinical observations/ rotations, to expose the learners to the clinical setting and patient care from a very early stage. During this program, they delve deeper into the domains of medicine within their study of theoretical and practical content, to foster the development of problem-solving and critical thinking skills while emphasizing treatment and clinical management of patients. During the final two semesters, learners will engage in an internship program while receiving special training sessions to help them better prepare for the licensing exams of their respective countries.
